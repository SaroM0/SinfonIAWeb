Mapping University Space with Humanoid Robot Pepper

2021
This project delves into the navigation domain, specifically exploring a robot's ability to generate maps of environments and self-locate. Focused on the humanoid robot Pepper, the study centers on understanding the RTAB-Map (Real-Time Appearance Based Mapping) library of ROS. This library employs a graph-based SLAM approach to map static scenes. The project involves studying and documenting various parameters of the library, conducting tests in controlled and open environments. The goal is to comprehend how the library influences map generation by inferring parameter values through experimentation.

Opera Learns To Play Line 4 Through Reinforcement Learning

2019
Juan José García led a project aiming to teach an pepper robot to devise game strategies for two-player board games, using the Line 4 game as the testing platform. The project applied reinforcement learning, specifically the Q-learning algorithm with a neural network, to learn from experience. Opponents were designed with varying levels of complexity in their plays, utilizing game theory principles like expectimax and minimax. The objective was for the Opera to excel in playing Line 4 by predicting the opponent's behavior several moves ahead.

Customer Service With Facial Recognition

2019
Adelaida Zuluaga's project focuses on enhancing a robot's ability to identify and recognize users locally through facial recognition techniques. The Viola-Jones algorithm was employed to detect individuals, achieving an impressive 98% accuracy within the robot's visual range. The Eigenfaces algorithm, coupled with SVMs one vs all, facilitated person recognition based on their names. Noteworthy is the algorithm's flexibility, allowing the robot to be trained on new classes and recognize new individuals during interactions. A significant contribution lies in providing the robot with local capabilities for identification and recognition, offering a reliable backup in case of cloud service failures.
